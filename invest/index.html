Common questions.

Is Aagman a broker? + −

No. It connects to your existing broker to support execution. It does not custody funds or replace your brokerage.

Does Aagman predict markets? + −

No. It does not attempt to forecast price direction. It helps you structure, test, and maintain your own trading logic under live conditions.

Why can't I just use ChatGPT, Claude, or Grok? + −

Because they don't operate inside a live market state. They respond to prompts. They don't track evolving positions, ingest real-time feeds as a trading system, compute indicators continuously, or stay synchronized with your execution account.

Aagman is connected to live market data, technical structures, and your brokerage environment. It works inside an active trading context — not outside it.

Does Aagman trade autonomously with my capital? + −

No. Aagman does not take discretionary control of your money. It connects to your brokerage account to support execution within your defined strategy. You retain control over capital and decision authority.

Is Aagman a signal or tip platform? + −

No. It does not provide stock tips, trade alerts, or copy-trading signals. It supports your own strategy and decision process.

What happens during high volatility? + −

Aagman continues to operate within the parameters you've defined. It does not override your strategy based on emotion or external noise.
